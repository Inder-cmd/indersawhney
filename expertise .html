Strategy only counts when it ships. This page unpacks how Inder approaches quality — the operating method, the outcomes it has produced, the AI-assisted practices he champions, and the tooling he reaches for at every layer of the delivery lifecycle.

THE OPERATING METHOD

Quality as a system, not a phase.

GATE 01

Strategy & risk first

Every program starts with a test strategy and risk-based testing model — deciding where quality investment matters most before a single case is written. Requirements are inspected for completeness, testability, and traceability up front.

GATE 02

Govern the pipeline

Standardized processes, centralized defect triage, and quality gates across multiple agile teams — so SIT, E2E, UAT regression, performance, DR, and cutover testing all run against the same definition of done.

GATE 03

Automate what repeats

CI/CD-integrated automation frameworks — Selenium, BDD, Cucumber, POM, Jenkins, Tosca — turn regression suites from bottlenecks into build steps, improving coverage, cycle time, and delivery confidence.

GATE 04

Report up, decide fast

Executive reporting through Power BI, qTest, ADO, and AQUA: risk dashboards, quality metrics, defect trend analysis, and release-readiness scorecards that let leadership make ship / no-ship calls with confidence.
